Speakers - Get up close with Canada's best in tech. Meet Silicon Valley in Toronto. Learn about the future of the industry.

Welcome to Imagine.

Canadian Undergraduate Technology Conference

Since 1999, CUTC (the Canadian Undergraduate Technology Conference) has sparked a passion for technology amongst 4500+ students across Canada.

Our mission is to be the most eminent, innovative, and stimulating forum for Canadian students, to learn about, explore, and influence the future of technology through interaction with each other, as well as leaders and visionaries from industry and academia.

At CUTC, you’ll see where the future of technology is headed, learn from the best, build things that matter and meet hustlers, makers and builders like you.

Who Are We?

An organising team with a passion to make impact. Alumni who are inspired to innovate.

We are entrepreneurs: members of our community have gone on to start over 30 companies including Nulogy, Polar Mobile, ThinkPanda, Neverfrost, Kira Talent and more.

We are leaders: others have risen to leadership positions in companies such as Google, Microsoft, Facebook, BCG and others.

We are academics: more have gone on to complete MBAs & PhDs from world-renowned schools such as Harvard, INSEAD, John Hopkins, and Wharton.

This year, we’re back to provide you the best place to indulge in all things tech.

Matt Wallaert

Behavioral Scientist | Microsoft (Ventures)

Matt Wallaert is a behavioral scientist working at the intersection of technology and human behavior. After several years in academia and two successful startup exits, he joined Microsoft, where he works across a variety of products and projects to design technology that helps people live happier, healthier lives, as well as the East Coast Lead for Microsoft Ventures, managing the company's relationships with prominent VCs and startups.

Wallaert maintains an active research program and is a frequent speaker on the science of behavior change and product design. He sits on the board a variety of startups and non-profits, and cultivates pro-social side projects like GetRaised.com, which has helped underpaid women ask for and earn millions of dollars in salary increases.

Matt's Talk:
Matt Wallaert is a white guy. A cowboy boot-wearing, born in the country, horse riding white guy. He is also a prominent male advocate and the scientist behind GetRaised, a free tool that has helped women earn over $2.3B in raises. In this session, Matt will talk about his continuing journey as a male advocate and how men in general can transform how they approach the issue of gender equity. Full of practical examples, the focus here is on the applied: this talk is about going beyond the moral imperative of equity to the daily living of an advocate.

Tom Redman

Lead Mobile Engineer | Buffer

Tom is the Mobile Engineering Lead at Buffer, where he leads the direction of the mobile vision, engineering team and products. His role is to identify the future of mobile both within and outside of Buffer, anticipating where it plays a role in the needs of Buffer's 3 million users.

Outside of Buffer, Tom spends his time reading, exercising and building several side projects, including a team directory for startups, an invoicing app for iOS, and a social photo printing service.

Tom's Talk:
This talk will focus on the past, present and future states of mobile products and development. Beginning with a look back to the early days of mobile, it will examine the progression of mobile technology in the past 10 years, and provide some speculation on the future.

It will also include a focus on working remotely in a technical environment, including details on tools and processes that facilitate efficient remote work with a team of 100 people. Using Buffer as an example, Tom will walk through daily life in a fully, 100% remote and transparent company.

FAQ

When and where is CUTC Imagine?

CUTC will be held on May 7-8th at the MaRS Innovation District in the heart of downtown Toronto.

What’s different at CUTC then say, at a hackathon?

At CUTC, it’s not just about hacking. Sure, you can get your hands dirty and create things during our events like the Microsoft Machine Learning Challenge or CGI’s Imagine Challenge; however, you also get to learn from the people in the trenches--entrepreneurs, pioneers in technology and the guys who build and maintain the tech we use everyday.

Will there be conference subsidies?

Yes! We'll be realeasing more information very soon. Watch out on our Facebook and Twitter for more updates!

Is a schedule available?

The schedule will be up very soon. We’re currently crafting something that gives you the best experience possible.

What am I getting for my $$?

We’ve been working hard to cut prices as much as possible to make sure cost doesn’t get in your way. Early Bird Tickets are $30 and Regular Tickets are $50. We also have a buddy deal where you can get $10 off when you buy 2 tickets together. You get access to keynotes, workshops, challenges, the Tech Expo, our pub night social, Bobby Motta’s world famous mentalist show (which normally costs $50 alone!) & delicious breakfast, lunch and dinner!

Where can I stay during the conference?

Need a place to stay for the weekend? We’ve got you covered! We've arranged a special deal with Mariott in downtown Toronto for just $45/night! All rooms will have 4 people. Just add an accommodation option on checkout. If a 4-star hotel stay with the CUTC fam ain't your thing, you can also try AirBnB, CouchSurfers or crashing at a friends place!

I still have questions!

Send 'em our way by shooting us a message on our Facebook page!

I'm coming from outside Toronto - how do I get there?

We'll be arranging FREE buses to/from schools with the most students registered. Currently, we anticipate providing free transportation to/from UWaterloo. We also have a deal with VIA Rail for 10% OFF if you book with discount code "13274".

Can I transfer my ticket to another person?

Absolutely, just send us a message on our Facebook page with the details and we’ll send that person a link to fill out a new registration form so they don’t have to use your name tag.

I’m interested in volunteering for CUTC, how can I get involved?

Applications for volunteers will be released in late March. Watch out on our Facebook and Twitter for more updates!
